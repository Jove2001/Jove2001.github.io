Why Study Information technology at RMIT Online?

What is my interest in IT?

I enjoy problem solving and using technology, and at it’s core IT is about using technology to find and implement solutions for human problems. Through the course of my work work career I became interested in systems analysis and process design, databases, and user experience.

I’m a musician, and I use IT tools to:

  • Record music - A home studio with a PC to record and edit music. With Virtual Studio Instruments (VSI) I can make any kind of sound I want.
  • Perform live - My devices communicate to each other via MIDI and give me a big sound for a one-man show. I use a Boss RC-505 loop machine, a Novation Ultranova synthesizer, a BeatBuddy drum machine, and a Boss GT-100 guitar FX unit.
  • Write sheet music or learn new music - GuitarPro ❤
  • Teach - I share lesson material, backing tracks, and sheet music with my students using Google Drive.


    • When did my interest in IT start?

    My dad bought a Commodore C64 when I was 8 or so, and I was immediately obsessed with it. Early on I mainly played games on it, but since it had a version of BASIC baked into the operating system I started to learn programming. I bought magazines with my pocket money that had pages of code that you typed in by hand. I noticed functions in the code and ‘borrowed’ them for my own programming projects.

    By the mid-1990’s I had Amiga computers which I used for school homework, games, coding, music production, and creating bitmap graphics.


    Was there a particular event or person that sparked my interest?

    Lenny is a friend of my Dad. He's an industrial electronics technician. Circa 1993 he introduced me to analog and FM sound synthesis, MIDI sequencing, and so many other things. He's an inspirational guy who travelled across the African continent in a Volkswagen Beetle during the 1970's.

    Any IT experience?

    I've always been the 'tech guy' in the workplace, but I used to call myself an 'expert user' rather than an 'expert in IT'. A about ten years ago I decided I wanted to understand what was happening under the hood of a modern PC, so I started learning Linux/GNU. With help from the lovely people in the Ubuntu Forums, I installed Ubuntu Linux on a laptop and learnt about using the command line and the principles of Linux and GNU. When I was confident enough, I built a NAS as a test bed for server based applications, and as a home for my digital life.

    My home IT setup looks like this:

    • A dual monitor desktop running Windows 10
    • A laptop running Linux Mint
    • A NAS running an Ubuntu LTS
    • A few Raspberry Pi SBC's

    Why did I choose to come to RMIT?

    I transitioned out of the corporate environment to a career as a saxophonist and guitarist around two years ago, but the current COVID crisis made me consider other options. I decided that studying the introduction courses for a Bachelor of Information Technology at RMIT would be a positive move for me.


    What do I expect to learn during my studies?

    My attitude to opportunity is similar to Richard Branson’s. In 2017 he said on his blog

    “If somebody offers you an amazing opportunity but you are not sure you can do it, say yes – then learn how to do it later.” 2
    This philosophy has a catch though: You can’t learn teamwork by yourself.

    I expect that by studying the Introduction to Information Technology course I will learn some of the skills of the IT professional and have a fuller understanding of the industry and the toolset. I’ve learnt a lot about website design and html by completing my first assignment. The second assignment task will help me learn more about collaborating with group members who may be physically located all over the world. IT professionals working in industry need to be able to communicate and collaborate effectively to achieve goals, so I’m learning how to use tools like Git. The third and fourth assignment tasks are about developing a project as a team. This will help me learn entrepreneurial skills like pitching a proposal to a client and developing a business presentation.
